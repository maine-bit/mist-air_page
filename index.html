Mist and Air

The mist, a veil that softly clings,
Drifts through the morning light like dreams.
The air, unseen yet ever near,
Whispers through the leaves, a ghostly cheer.
Though they wander the same silent paths,
They touch but never truly grasp.

The mist, heavy with secrets untold,
Hangs over fields, a story cold.
The air, forever light and free,
Dances around it, wild and unseen.
Their fates entwined, yet always apart,
One grounded in presence, the other in heart.

The mist, a fleeting, fragile breath,
Lingers on the edge of life and death.
It craves the air’s elusive form,
To hold it close, to feel its warmth.
But each attempt, though tender, fails,
As love dissolves in vapor trails.

And so they drift, both near and far,
Like distant echoes of a star.
Bound by fate, yet worlds apart,
Two aching halves of the same heart.
In their endless dance, we learn anew,
Some connections are felt, but never true.

Still, they yearn for what they’ll never know,
To merge and feel the other’s glow.
But some things in life are destined to be
Parallel forces, longing, yet free.
In their silent ballet, we see ourselves,
Two souls adrift, on different shelves.

In their longing, something stays,
A quiet sorrow that never decays.
For though they part, they leave behind
A trace of love, in heart and mind.
